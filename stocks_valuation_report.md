## How to Use This Table

- **Best Possible Cheap Price Range** is the accumulation zone with meaningful margin of safety relative to intrinsic value and/or historical median multiples. Stocks may not reach this range; context is in the justification.
- **Business Justification** covers: (a) valuation basis for the range, (b) key management guidance from Q4 FY26 concall, (c) primary growth catalyst, (d) principal risk.
- Sector ordering: Capital Goods → Financial Services → IT & Technology → Healthcare → Consumer/Energy/Other.

## Key Observations

- **Financial Services stocks dominate the near-cheap or at-cheap zone.** Among the 39 names, the Financial Services cluster — spanning banking (Federal Bank, IDFC First, RBL Bank, YES Bank), NBFCs (Five-Star, SG Finserve, Ugro Capital, Home First, India Shelter), and capital markets infrastructure (CAMS, CDSL, BSE, KFINTECH, Nuvama, HDFCAMC, 360ONE) — collectively offers the highest number of entries where the best-value range is within 5–20% of the current market price, implying the sector has already partly corrected to reasonable valuations after the FY26 mid-cap financial de-rating cycle.

- **Capital Goods and EMS names require the largest corrections to reach cheap zones, but carry the highest catalyst density.** Stocks like Amber Enterprises (~30% correction needed), Dixon (~20%), Kaynes (~33%), and Ramkrishna Forgings (~20–25%) currently trade well above their best-value ranges, and the market is already pricing in FY27–28 execution. The investment case for this group is strong — India's PLI, semiconductor mission, and EMS content-per-device trends are structural — but patience is required as seasonal losses, capex-cycle costs, and integration overhangs are the natural de-rating catalysts that create these entry windows.

- **IT and Technology names are bifurcated: mid-tier services offer deep value, while travel-tech and automotive ER&D require contrarian conviction.** Coforge (at ₹1,100–1,200, approximately 45% below current) and Newgen (at ₹420–480, approximately 35% below current) represent the deepest PE-to-growth discounts in the IT cluster; KPIT Technologies at ₹650–780 is the most contrarian call in the group, trading near its 5-year valuation lows on OEM spending cyclicality fears despite no structural deterioration in its CASE megatrend moat.

- **Textile and export-oriented names (GOKEX, ICIL, Apex Frozen Foods) are in a tariff-driven event window.** The best-value ranges for all three are calibrated around an assumption that FY26 margin troughs and tariff-resolution uncertainty persist. The India-US trade deal and EU FTA timelines are the single swing variable — materialisation in H2 FY27 would rapidly push all three well above their fair value zones, while a delay or reversal of tariff concessions would keep them range-bound in the cheap zone for longer, making these the highest tactical beta positions in the portfolio.

- **Healthcare and insurance represent the most under-owned re-rating opportunity.** Star Health's cheap zone (₹420–510) is the only insurance name in the list and it is positioned at the beginning of a multi-year combined ratio normalisation journey — the FY26 CR of 98.8% improving to sub-95% represents a structural earnings multiplier that the 68x TTM PE headline number entirely obscures, while Shaily Engineering's GLP-1 optionality and STARHEALTH's improving claims mix make this the sub-cluster most likely to re-rate sharply from a single confirming data point (a take-or-pay pen contract or two consecutive quarters of sub-96% CR).
